## BACHELOR’S THESIS

Study on Rainfall Interception Capacity of Salix Psammophila Shrub


Purpose and Meaning: Rainfall is the main water source in desert dryland, and water is an important factor affecting plant growth. In the process of vegetation rainfall redistribution, interception is the most important part. The interception ability and influence of Salix psammophila shrub under precipitation redistribution were discussed in order to provide theoretical basis and data basis for planting and management of Salix psammophila in sandy land.
Analysis Method: Taking Salix psammophila shrubs with different slopes in Maowusu Desert Experimental Site as the research object, through the field measurement experiment in rainy season (June-September), the penetration rainfall and stem flow after rainfall are measured, and through calculation and correlation analysis, the influence of interception of Salix psammophila shrubs on precipitation redistribution is explored. Regression analysis is used to judge the influence of rainfall, rainfall interception and other parameters on interception, and the equation is fitted according to the analysis data.
Conclusion: Through the correlation analysis of the interception of Salix psammophila on a gentle, middle, and slope with total rainfall and penetrating rainfall, it can be seen that the total rainfall has a stronger influence, and the fitting formula obtained by studying the correlation of total rainfall is more feasible.
